### H-COVERAGE-001: Incomplete Mutex poison recovery — 20+ unwrap() calls remain across 5 crates

**Severity**: high  
**Kind**: route-fix  
**File(s)**:
- `moni-rs/crates/moni-factor-registry/src/lib.rs` lines 79, 91, 96, 107, 132, 138 (6 sites)
- `moni-rs/crates/moni-qlib-adapter/src/lib.rs` lines 164, 232 (2 sites)
- `moni-rs/crates/moni-factor-adapter/src/lib.rs` lines 82, 86, 95 (3 sites)
- `moni-rs/crates/moni-replay/src/lib.rs` lines 87, 91, 103, 235 (4 sites)
- `moni-rs/crates/moni-post-close/src/lib.rs` lines 39, 73 (2 sites)

**Finding**: The same cascade-panic-on-poison vulnerability that MM-M001/MM-M003 fixed in moni-strategy remains unfixed in 5 sibling crates (17 non-test `.lock().unwrap()` call sites). Any panic while holding a Mutex in these crates will permanently poison it, causing every subsequent caller to also panic — cascading engine crash identical to what was fixed in sessions.rs/strategy.rs.

**Exclusions verified**: `moni-core/src/config.rs` lines 663, 705, 715, 773 are inside `#[cfg(test)]` — acceptable for test code.

**Remediation**: Apply the same `unwrap_or_else(|p| { eprintln!(...); p.into_inner() })` pattern to all 17 remaining production call sites. Mechanical transformation, low risk. Can be batched as a single route-fix per crate or one combined commit.
